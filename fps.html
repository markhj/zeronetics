FPS (frames per second)

One of the key metrics in game rendering that almost everyone is familiar with is FPS, or frames per second.

ðŸ’» FPS

FPS is an expression of how well the hardware and the game's code perform in unison. The higher the number, the better.

Essentially, it calculates how many frames the machine is able to render per second.

To make sure objects move consistently across the screen, across devices, it's important that you're familiar with delta time.

To retrieve the FPS, call ZEN::Game::getFPS.

fps_int currentFPS = game.getFPS();

However, FPS can — and often is — artificially limited to match the monitor's frame rate, and thereby reduce the risk of artifacts, such as "_screen tear_." It's therefore not guaranteed to be a waterproof expression for how well your game is performing.

When profiling — knowing how well your game loop actually performs &mdash you might want to use ZEN::Game::getRealRenderTime instead.

Note
This function measures the time from the beginning of the render loop and until the scene is fully rendered. But it cuts off before the renderer swaps its buffer.
